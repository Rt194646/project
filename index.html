Change Over Time: Video Games and Public Perception (1995–2010)

Introduction Claim Timeline Major Changes Public Perception Sources

Introduction

Between 1995 and 2010, video game graphics evolved dramatically—from pixelated art to cinematic realism. This project explores how those changes affected how the public saw games: from “just toys” to a powerful storytelling medium.

Claim

Between 1995 and 2010, improvements in video game graphics played a major role in changing public perception, helping games be seen not just as entertainment but as legitimate culture and even art.

Key Evidence Timeline

1995

Doom used 2.5D pixel graphics that were innovative at the time. But games were still seen as simple and childish.

2000

Halo: Combat Evolved showed how far 3D design had come—smooth environments, AI enemies, and immersive worlds helped games grow in seriousness.

2010

Red Dead Redemption used HD graphics, motion capture, and complex open-world design. It made players feel like they were in a movie—changing how society viewed games forever.

Major Changes

  • Technology: Graphics cards, 3D engines, HD displays, motion capture.
  • Design: Games became more narrative-focused and cinematic.
  • Audience: Older, broader player base with new expectations.
  • Debate: Concerns over violence, addiction, realism, and impact on youth.

Public Perception Timeline

  • 1995: “Games are just for fun or for kids.”
  • 2000: “Games are cool and popular.”
  • 2010: “Games can be serious, artistic, or even dangerous.”

Most valuable takeaway: I learned how fast technology can shape society’s opinions, and how games became more than entertainment.
