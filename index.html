The Evolution of Video Game Graphics (1995–2010)

Tracking graphical progress and public perception through iconic milestones.

Doom (1995) – early 3D FPS with flat sprites over fake-3D environments.

Halo: Combat Evolved (2001) – introduction of 3D polygons and lighting, more immersive.

Red Dead Redemption (2010) – cinematic visuals, dynamic weather, realistic lighting and textures.

RDR town & train combo – detailed environment and storytelling visuals.

1. Doom (1995)

Flat sprites, 2.5D environments. Health/armor HUD and pixelated demons. Public saw groundbreaking FPS visuals.

2. Halo: Combat Evolved (2001)

Fully 3D polygon rendering, dynamic lighting, realistic environments. FPS became immersive and cinematic.

3. Red Dead Redemption (2010)

Cinematic scale, detailed textures, day–night cycle, weather effects. Public viewed these visuals as near-movie quality.
