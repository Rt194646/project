Introduction

This website explores how video game graphics and public perception changed from 1995 to 2010. It covers technological advances and how society's view of games shifted over time.

1995: Early 3D and Pixel Art

Games like Doom and Tekken 2 used pixelated graphics and simple 3D models. At the time, these visuals were groundbreaking, but they now appear quite basic.

2000: Graphic Revolution

Games like The Sims and Halo introduced more realistic characters and environments. Developers used improved 3D engines and lighting techniques.

2010: Cinematic Realism

By 2010, games like Red Dead Redemption and Mass Effect 2 used motion capture and HD textures. These titles blurred the line between games and movies.
